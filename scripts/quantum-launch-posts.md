## Reddit — r/QuantumComputing

**Title:** `We used a 20-qubit QAOA circuit to optimize AI agent defence thresholds — outperformed a genetic algorithm by +3.1% fitness`

```
We ran 5 quantum experiments on an NVIDIA DGX Spark (PennyLane lightning.gpu + cuQuantum 26.01.0) for optimizing multi-agent AI security systems. Sharing results for feedback from the quantum computing community.

**The problem:** The NAIL Institute's Pathology Collider has 12 tuneable defence thresholds that protect AI agent teams from adversarial attacks (memory poisoning, consensus cartels, etc.). We needed to optimize these in a 12-dimensional space with complex inter-parameter correlations.

**Phase 1 — QAOA Threshold Optimizer (20 qubits, 6 StronglyEntanglingLayers)**

- 360 trainable weights, Adam optimizer (lr=0.08), adjoint differentiation
- 150 steps, 790s total (5.27s/step)
- Result: fitness 0.9383 vs GA baseline 0.9071 (+3.44%)
- Accuracy: 79.3% vs 67.4% (+11.9 pp)
- Corruption: 2.2% vs 5.7% (−3.5 pp)

The interesting finding: the entangling layers discovered a correlation between `memory_decay_rate` and `alert_cooldown` that the GA's crossover/mutation could not access. The quantum optimizer adopted a qualitatively different strategy — strict memory gating + lenient alert timing.

**Phase 2a — Hybrid QNN Pathology Classifier (8 qubits)**
- PyTorch + PennyLane hybrid: 84.2% accuracy (classical: 96.7%)
- 100% accuracy on 3/6 pathology classes (Normal, Memory Corruption, EDoS)
- Class-selective quantum advantage — QNN excels where features have distinct quantum signatures

**Phase 2b — Quantum Kernel SVM (8 qubits)**
- ZZFeatureMap kernel: 54.2% — underperformed. Documented honestly.
- Likely needs different feature maps and more training data

**Phase 3 — Robustness Certification (12 qubits)**
- Quantum random sampling for adversarial perturbation vectors
- Gold certified: 100% coverage at fitness ≥ 0.90 (99% CI: 97.4%–100%)
- Quantum adversarial vectors more diverse than classical uniform sampling

**Phase 4 — QAOA Multi-Agent Coordinator (20 qubits)**
- QUBO formulation for 6-agent × 8-task allocation
- Found optimal assignment in 23s, matching classical brute-force

**Phase 5 — Quantum Risk Scoring (6 qubits)**
- VQC: 85% accuracy on 4-tier classification (classical: 100%)
- Monotonic tier separation validated, but compressed dynamic range

All code + results: https://github.com/NAIL-INSTITUTE-FOR-AGENTIC-SECURITY/ave-database/tree/main/quantum

Happy to discuss circuit design choices and methodology.
```
